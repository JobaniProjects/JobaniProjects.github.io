MOD & TESSELE

Phase 1



Origami | Star Pattern and Modification



For the modification of the petra star I took an individual piece and inserted it into another piece that had a crevice in it. Then I followed the
same pattern for all the other pieces, flipping some around to have a bit of change on the patter. The result gave me a heart shape. This was
my first attempt and a happy accident.



2D Tesselation & Modification



I followed the triangle tesselation tutorial with my own design to get a wavy tesselation. I found some interesting connections, and I had fun
creating this from start to finish. It was therapeutic.




Group Project



Our group decided to choose the arrows, stars, and circles as our common shape. For our base shape we chose
the square. With these common shapes, it was easier for us to find parts that would combine with one another.
There were no gaps and all our shapes fit perfectly.




Phase 2

Kirigami Model & Fastener




For my 3D fastener, I wanted the end result to look like a sphere/globe. I did 1-inch cuts from the tip of the edge where they can
interlock with one another. I found this design through a youtube tutorial. The end result was a success. It looks like a lantern
near the light.


Phase 3

Goldilocks Laser Tests



First Design Trial




For this trial we decided to use foamboard. There were burns on the foamboard but overall the pieces were intact. They
securely fastened just as planned.





Second Design Trial








For my first test in designing my fastener, I decided that the end result look like a small cubed house. My base
shape was squares and one rectangle. The purpose of the rectangle was to see if it would fasten perfectly to the
other shapes.

Artist Statement

The first idea that our group agreed on was Vanessa's idea of our shapes relating to houses
that are stacked to one another. This idea reminded me of the building structure of the favelas
or certain buildings in Mexico, which also inspired me to create my design to look like a
rectangular building. I decided to make my shapes blue similar to the colorful buildings in
Mexico. We were able to achieve our goal to successfully fasten our pieces together and create
a colorful village.

Improvements and Success



Due to the pandemic rules and regulations, our group's communication was held mostly online,
making communication difficult at first, but over time it improved. We all contributed and worked
hard on our designs and pieces. All of our pieces connected with one another and managed
to finish the pieces and video documentation on time despite complications with the laser
cutter being unavailable and delaying our progress. Overall we did our best, we did our part, and
our end goal was met.
