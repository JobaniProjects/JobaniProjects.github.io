Q1) Given the Wikipedia definations on data and information, what is your own take on this and specfically what is the difference between data and information?

After reading about information and data, I understand now that data is just numbers or symbols that were given meaning to track progress of a person or thing. Information resolves uncertainty. The difference between those two is that data is just numbers or symbols that were observed and recorded, while information is the answer to a question.


Q2) From the Manovich reading, what is the difference between visualization and mapping?

Mapping is changing information to different dimensions and having better control of what can be viewed. Visualization is visual representation of data; graphs and charts fall under the category of visualization. Visualization isn't as flexible at showing information as mapping.


Q3) When Manovich states that visualization is the anti-sublime art what is he referring to? Do you agree with this notion?

He is referring to when something is incomprehensible and grand, visualization reduces it to simple graphs on a screen where human beings are now capable to understand it. I disagree with this notion. I believe something grand can be shown visually with also keeping status of it being grand by presenting the visualization in an interesting, unique way.
