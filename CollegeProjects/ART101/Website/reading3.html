Q1) From the Manovich reading, give 2 examples of user interface with a piece of technology that has a performative action or theatrical behavior. This is in context with the ideas credited to Brenda Laurel that Lev mentions in the article. They can be electronic/digital or analog but NOT a computer, video game console or smart phone. Explain why these are good examples.

One example of user interface with performative action would be cameras with an LED display. When you turn on the camera, the display would turn on and an animation of the camera's logo pops up, and depending on the brand, some also add special effects to these welcome startups, demonstrating theatrical behavior. This startup is cool, but not necessary. There are cameras that do not have a welcome startup and they go straight to the point and are ready to be used in an instant. Another great example would be television. When you turn on the TV, an animation of the brand will appear right in the middle of the screen and sometimes a unique startup sound will be heard. This display is not necessary because I already know what television brand it is, but it does alert me when someone turns on the TV with its sounds and the startup animation is welcoming and familiar.


Q2) Also from the Manovich reading, Lev uses the term Gesamtkunstwerk. What does this term mean and how is he referring to the aestheticization of information tools?

What Lev means when he refers to Gesamtkunstwerk is when something engages directly the five senses. Technology can be called Gesamtkunstwerk. This term is referring to the aestheticization of information tools because most of the time there are features that capture our attention by engaging our senses and keeps us thinking about the tool. These type of features are not necessary but they do help to distinguish your tool and help captivate the audience to get your tool.


Q3) From the RadioLab Podcast, the guest podcast artist is sharing his radio show called 99% invisible. The first piece is about sound design. What would you describe the role of the sound designer and how does it relate to this idea of theatre and performance?

The role of the sound designer is to add sound wherever sound is needed to amplify the mood and energy. It relates to the idea of theatre and performance because sound is used to entertain you and get you attached to the technology. By adding sound it creates a fun environment and experience.


Q4)From the Skeuomorphism vs. Flat Design article, what are the philosophical and visual differences between Skeuomorphism vs. Flat Design and how might this also relate to this idea of theatre and gamification or aesthetic based event described in the Lev Article?

The difference is that Skeuomorphism tries to replicate designs that are familar to the people to get them comfortable with the product and to easily recognize it. Flat design goes a different route and instead focuses on simple, minimal, and clean design. This relates to the idea of theatre and gamification because the design itself lures your sense of sight and then evokes emotion based on your opinion on the design.


Q5) Based on the Manovich reading, and following up on Q.1 ., As a group, find some images of 2 or more different examples of technologies that embodies for you the authors idea of how interfaces to early electronics or machine systems were designed in the past. Specifically look for examples from 1950's,60's, and 70's, (..not 1980's and 90's...). Explain why these are good examples. Then list 2 or more more images of examples of technology that embody the authors ideas of how interfaces are today. Again, explain why these are good examples. Please include images and text description for each example you are refering to. At least 2 images minimum per person from a group. For example a group of 3 should have 6 images. Share these images as a group in discord, and then publish your images on your readings website.

The first image is a 1978 Speak & Spell toy, one of the earliest handheld electronic devices with a visual display and the first to use the first single-chip voice synthesizer. This is a great example because it has theatrical behavior; when starting up the toy, it first plays a playful sound followed by a male robotic voice telling you to spell out a word. Each button you press the robot voice will pronounce each time. The second image is a Cozmo bot, a most recent piece of technology that demonstrates characteristics of theatrical behavior. When Cozmo bot is booted up, the display will show two eyeball dots slowly opening and "looking" around its surroundings. Then it starts to speak by making noises that represent and mimic tone and emotion.



Q6) As a group, please come up with examples of two different kind of technology that normally don't have this interface design or 'consideration' mentioned in the reading and podcast, but could benefit from this 'theatrifaction' or 'gamification' idea of its interface. Please describe what would you suggest doing to it to change it.

Vending machines could benefit from theatrifaction. When I use a vending machine, it never seems like a great experience using the machine. You insert your money in the machine, pick your drink/snack, and the machine gives it to you. There is no connection between the machine and the user. Adding a soft gingle or music like in an elelvator while you pick your beverage/snack will make the experience delightful.
