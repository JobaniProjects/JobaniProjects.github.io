READING #2 | SITE SPECIFIC


1) What are the differences between site specific art work and the idea of art interventions?

Site specific art work is primarily focused on location and it is created to exist in a specific
environment. Art intervention is artwork that focuses on previously existing art, places, audiences,
or circumstances. So site specific has a particular space to display work while art intervention uses
an existing venue.


2) What are the similarities and differences regarding the work of Walter De Maria and Banksy?
Include your thoughts on their idea of site specificness and interventions.

Something similar both Banksy and Walter have is that both artists have their art displayed in locations
outside of a normal gallery space. Some differences between them is the way they present their art.
Walter's work doesn't stand out as much; it's simple and almost minimalistic. Banksy places his work in
obscure public areas for all to see; it stands out more. This also means that Walter focuses on site-specific
art and Banksy focuses on interventions.


3) What are some of the most common creative tactics that all the 'signs' must shares? What must
sign have to remain relevant? We are talking about signs that both in the practical context as well
as signs that function as art.

Some of the most common creative tactics that all the signs share would be minimal words, vibrant colors,
picture, and bolded letters. It stays relevant because it is very useful for drawing attention to you. It also allows
us to communicate a message to one another.


4) What kind of art, not shown here, have you seen that was made in response to its location, its
environment, a specific event or situation. Please elaborate on its description, provide images if possible.

Artist Ervin Loránth Hervé created an impressive sculpture called “Popped Up” that depicts a giant man
crawling out of the earth. It uses the grass that is part of the environment to create that illusion. This is an
example of site-specific installation.
