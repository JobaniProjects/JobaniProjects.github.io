Be water my friend. -- Bruce Lee

Q1) What would be a good definition of how any kind of artist can use an algorithm in their art.

Any kind of artist can use algorithms in their art because algorithms are a sequence of commands that get triggered to get an outcome that can be either simple or complex, which allows the artist to go further beyond the capabilities of their art.


Q2) Give 3 specific examples of how you might already use algorithms in your own art work.

My first artwork example that uses algorithms is a videogame group project that is called "Earth Defenders". It used algorithms to change scenes, move characters, and tell a story in chronological order. My second example would be a short movie created in one of my classes. The scenes were planned and sketched on a shot sheet, and I recorded the scenes in the same order as the shot sheet. My last example is creating a digital artwork for my final. When creating it, I broke down the process into steps. For the first step, I come up with at least 3 concepts that I would like the project to be about. Then I pick one that I favor the most, that is possible with the given time, and that is in my skill level. I start creating the artwork and call it my rough draft. I work on it again, making edits, calling it my second draft. For the final step, I examine it and try to put myself into the perspective of others, to determine if it is worthy enough to be turned in. These are the steps that took place in order to create the digital artwork for my final. I also use this algorithm on any other digital work that I have done.


Q3) Look at the 6 artists/examples above and please describe for at least 3 of them, 'where' do you think the art is and how would you talk about the process of the 'art' vs the art 'artifact'. For example, If an artist is using computers to generate art, where is the art? Is it in the software program or the product it makes? PLease label each one, then eloborate on the question.

Harold Cohen and his "other self" called AARON both work together to make pieces of art, but Harold working with his code self and creating artwork with it is where the art is.

Miguel Nobrega's outcome of what his code can do is where I think the art is at. There is extreme precision, detail, and quickness when the machine starts to draw, only being possible by the algorithm. Every final outcome will be perfect and cool.

Turux's ideas of what he wants his tools to do is where the art is. He creates tools that are interactive and unique to his own style. He has created many original tools that never cease to amaze and he has made it simple for other users to use his tools. With a stroke of the mouse you instantly get the max potential of that tool.


Q4) What is in example of a well known artist that has an iconic style to you?. Please create a basic 'ingredient list' and of the formal steps of a process that might create their style. In addition find an image of this artist you can find on the internet, that you can share in discord.

Banksy has an iconic style that is known by every street artist. Basic ingredients list for Banksy.
Step 1: Gather your stencils, white spray paint, and black spray paint.
Step 2: Find a building.
Step 3: Spray paint either a human or animals that reflects a political message.
Step 4: Remain anonymous.

How do they describe what the tasks are and why are their styles different?

The pizza recipe gives a thorough tutorial and goes as far as to tell you the other options you have for ingredients. Also, the examples are of real people and not of cartoon. For the tooth brushing instructions, they show the examples in flat design. Also, they give extra pieces of information related to toothbrushing. Rock, paper, scissors, lizards, Spock, explains hand movements through a looping video example and the instructions go in depth on the strategy of the game. For the plane instructions, it did not go to hyper detail on the process of making an airplane. It assumes that you know what to do with the information it doesn't provide. The instructions on writing instructions was really detailed. It gave advice to improve and examples that supported its claim.
